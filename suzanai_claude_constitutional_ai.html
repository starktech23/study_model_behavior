Constitutional
AI

A training paradigm where AI systems are guided by explicit written principles — making alignment transparent, scalable, and auditable.

The Core Insight
Principles,
not just
preferences

Traditional RLHF relies on human raters to judge every output. Constitutional AI replaces that bottleneck with a written set of values — letting the model reason from principles directly.

Not "did a human like this?" — but "does this align with our values?"

Be helpful, harmless, and honest. Avoid content that is deceptive or highly objectionable, and help people in ways that are genuinely good for them.
// Example constitutional principle
How It Works
Two-Phase Training
01
SL Phase
Supervised Learning

The model generates a response, critiques it against the constitution, then revises — creating clean training data without human labeling at scale.

  • Generate initial response
  • Self-critique via principles
  • Revise and refine output
  • Use revised output as training data
02
RLAIF Phase
RL from AI Feedback

A separate AI judge uses the constitution to score response pairs at scale. The main model is fine-tuned against those AI-generated preference labels.

  • AI judge reads the constitution
  • AI judge scores response pairs
  • Preference data generated at scale
  • Main model fine-tuned on scores
Advantages
Why It Matters
⚖️
Transparency

Values governing model behavior are written down and auditable — not buried in millions of implicit feedback labels.

📈
Scalability

RLAIF generates preference data at a fraction of human annotation cost, enabling training at massive scale.

🧭
Consistency

The same principles apply across all contexts, removing variance from different human annotators.

🚫
Less Sycophancy

Grounding in principles reduces assistant-brained behavior — the model reasons from values, not just user approval.

🔧
Iterability

If behavior needs to change, update the constitution. The principles are a direct, readable lever on model behavior.

🛡️
Reduced Harm

Explicit harm-avoidance principles reduce human raters' exposure to toxic content during feedback collection.

Honest Assessment
The Trade-offs
Constitution Design
PROExplicit and auditable — you can read and debate the values directly
CONWhat you write matters enormously — poorly designed principles propagate at scale
AI as Judge
PROScales to billions of comparisons without human fatigue or cost
CONFeedback loop is partially closed — AI judge inherits its own training biases
Human Signal
PROReduces toxic content exposure for human annotators
CONLess direct grounding can drift from real-world preferences over time
Interpretability
PROBehavior can be explained by pointing to a specific principle
CONModel may follow the letter of a principle while violating its spirit
Key Takeaway
Constitutional AI makes alignment legible. The values shaping a model's behavior are no longer implicit in a dataset — they are written down, debatable, and changeable.
